Global Food System Index

Expert Survey on Issue Weighting

Introduction

Purpose of the Survey

This survey supports the construction of a Global Food System Index by collecting expert judgments on the relative importance (weights) of key food system issues.

The analytical framework is grounded in the Five Eater Needs (5E), which define the essential conditions a food system must satisfy to support human wellbeing for a global population of 8 billion people.

How to Answer

  • Rate the importance of issues at the same analytical level
  • Scores range from 1 (lowest) to 10 (highest)
  • Assess importance relative to other issues in the same group
  • Scores will be normalized and converted into weights
  • There are no correct or incorrect answers

Part II – Third-Level Issues

A. Food Sufficiency

Third-Level IssueExplanationImportance (1–10)
Resource Constraints Limitations arising from climate conditions, water availability, land resources, soil quality, and other natural resource constraints affecting food production capacity.
Efficiency Limitations Inefficiencies in production, storage, processing, transport, and distribution that reduce the effective availability of food.
Demand Pressures Pressures arising from population growth, dietary transitions, and competing non-food uses of agricultural products.
External Inflows and Aid Dependence Reliance on food imports, food aid, or external supply channels to meet domestic food sufficiency needs.

B. Food Diversity

Third-Level IssueExplanationImportance (1–10)
Domestic Diversity Constraints Constraints on domestic production and availability of diverse food categories, limiting dietary variety.
Import and Introduction Constraints Barriers to importing or introducing diverse food types due to trade, infrastructure, policy, or logistical limitations.

C. Food Safety

Third-Level IssueExplanationImportance (1–10)
Food Contamination Risks Risks related to biological, chemical, and physical contamination throughout production, processing, storage, transport, and retail.
Food Deterioration Risks Risks related to food spoilage, enzymatic degradation, and chemical deterioration affecting food safety and quality.

D. Food and Longevity

Third-Level IssueExplanationImportance (1–10)
Diet-Related Health Problems Health impacts associated with underconsumption, overconsumption, unbalanced diets, unsafe food, or inappropriate eating patterns.
Diet-Related Longevity Outcomes Long-term effects of dietary patterns on life expectancy, ageing trajectories, and population-level health outcomes.

E. Food System Sustainability

Third-Level IssueExplanationImportance (1–10)
Social and Institutional Issues Governance, legal frameworks, public education, economic systems, and social conflicts shaping food system stability.
Ecological and Environmental Issues Environmental degradation, pollution, resource depletion, biodiversity loss, and ecological damage arising from food system activities.
